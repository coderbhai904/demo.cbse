The CBSE is a national-level education board in India that manages public and private schools. The CBSE was established in 1929 to promote cooperation and integration between states in secondary education. The CBSE's headquarters are in New Delhi, India, and its official languages are Hindi and English.
Here are some facts about the CBSE:
The CBSE sets the curriculum for grades 9–12 for affiliated schools.
The CBSE conducts the All India Secondary School Examination (AISSE) at the end of grade 10 and the All India Senior School Certificate Examination (AISSCE) at the end of grade 12.
The CBSE's exams are known for being application-based rather than theoretical. The CBSE's aim is to help students develop confidence, acquire knowledge, and be able to assess their strengths and weaknesses.
The CBSE mandates that all affiliated schools follow the National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) curriculum.
